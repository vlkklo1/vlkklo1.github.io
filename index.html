Las Vegas tourism officials say the spike is a sign of improved conditions in the valley.

The city's tourism association, which hosts a "Vegas Spirit Guide" for visitors to the Valley, says the number of visitors this summer "remains above the highest it's been in years."

But, city spokeswoman Monica Johnson says, it's not always so, as visitors are now often delayed by bad weather.

"It does make it difficult for businesses, families, and families that have moved down here for summer vacations," she said

The number of tourists in July 2014 also "saw a significant decrease" compared to the July 2013, Johnson said. July 2013 peaked at more than 9 million visitors, while in July 2014, it reached about 7.4 million.

"It's a result of people coming home from overseas, taking in the resorts here in Las Vegas," Johnson said. "It's just not a good time to go, so we've had fewer visitors during the holiday season."
